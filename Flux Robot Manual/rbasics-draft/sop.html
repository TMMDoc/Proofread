Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

  • The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish guidelines for the safe and effective operation of the Fanuc robot.

  • This SOP applies to all technicians who will be operating the robot.

  • It is the technician’s responsibility to adhere to the guidelines set forth in this SOP and to ensure that all personnel operating the robot are following these guidelines.

Safety

  1. Do not stand near the robot or inside the safety fence when the operating mode is set to T2 or Auto.

  2. Do not leave the robot unattended while it is in operation, especially when its working in auto mode.

  3. Safety shoes and a helmet must be always worn while operating or working with robot.

  4. Ensure that no person is inside the safety fence while the robot is operating.

Pre-Operational checks and Error diagnosis

Operating mode of robot

  1. Robot should be in T1 mode (reduced speed).

    T1 mode is for normal R&D operation.

  2. To operate the robot with full speed (100%) during bending, set the operating mode as T2.

Teach pendant E-stop

Release the E-stop switch located at top right corner of the teach pendant.

Reset the fence in T1 or T2 mode

The fence needs to be reset when you see the message “Fence status abnormal”.

  1. Press shift and DIAG button (left down)

  2. Press soft button RES_CHN1

  3. Select Yes

  4. Press Reset button

Reset the fence in AUTO mode

The fence needs to be closed when you get this error message “Fence open” since robot can’t be operated with fence open in AUTO mode.

  1. Close both the fence doors simultaneously.

  2. Press reset button.

Deadman switch released

  • The Deadman switch consists of two positions, to operate the robot operator needs to press and hold in first position continuously till the operating process ends.

  • Pressing the second position will also trigger the Dead switch released error.

    1. Hold the Deadman switch located at the back of the teach pendant in first position.

    2. Press the reset button, and the fault LED should turn off.

Emergency

  • In case of any emergency, Press emergency stop button on either teach pendant or robot control panel.

  • This will abruptly stop all Robot operations and movements; no operation will be performed in Emergency stop condition.

  • Make sure the robot’s surrounding area is clear before moving it again.

Maintenance

  1. Keep the area around the robot clean and free from objects.

  2. Make use of LOTO (Lock Out - Tag Out), when robot is in maintenance work.

Requirements

  1. All personnel must undergo comprehensive training on the correct use of the robot, including:

    • Proper operation procedures

    • Safety protocols

    • Maintenance requirements

  2. Only trained and authorized personnel are permitted to operate the robot.

  3. All personnel who will be operating the robot are required to read and fully understand this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) prior to using the robot.

  4. It is essential that no steps are missed during robot operation to ensure safety and proper functioning.
