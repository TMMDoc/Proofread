Best Practices

Best Practices

  1. Gripper Selection: Select the appropriate gripper for the part to be picked up. Consider factors such as the size and thickness of the material when selecting a gripper.

    • Part size less than 200 X 200: Use Combigripper

    • Part size greater than 300 X 300: Use Vaccum gripper

  2. Tool Selection: Add appropriate die and punch according the part and the bend requirements.

  3. Tool Position: Move the tools (punch and die) and observe navigation bar. Errors will get minimized at a certain place while moving the tools. Fix that position as the tool position.

    • To move the tools, click and drag them.

    • To move both the punch and die together, hold the shift key and select both tools and drag the red arrow in the middle of the tool.

      image:

  4. Part Alignment: Align the part to the corner of the pickup pallet.

    • To align the part, click on the pickup pallet and select "Align corner" in the pickup panel.

  5. Bend line parallel to machine: When determining the pickup position, make sure that the 1st bend line of the part is parallel to the bending machine.

    • To determine the pickup position, click on the part and start adjusting its position by changing the position and angle values in pickup panel.

  6. Suction should face the bend line: During 1st bend, ensure that the bend line 1 of the part is above the gripper’s suction cup when using a vacuum gripper. Also, ensure that the suction cup is not too close to the bend line to avoid collision during bending.

  7. Regrip: Consider adding a regrip if the part is not securely held by the gripper after the first bend. This can help ensure that the part is properly positioned for subsequent bends and can prevent errors during bending.

    • To add a regrip, click on the respective bend sequence in the navigation bar and select "Add regrip" in the bend panel.

  8. Avoid redundant regrips: When there are multiple bends, avoid adding unnecessary regrips as they can increase cycle time.

    • To avoid redundant regrips, try changing the tool position and see if the part is securely held by the gripper after the first bend. If it is, then a regrip may not be necessary.

  9. Error Resolution Sequence: When resolving errors, it is generally best to follow the sequence of the navigation bar. Fixing errors out of sequence may introduce new errors, so it is best to resolve them in the order they appear.

  10. Kinematic error: A kinematic error means the robot cannot reach a waypoint with the current robot posture (joint configuration), even if there is no direct collision.

    • Check the highlighted operation in the navigation bar and open the waypoint that shows the robot error (for example, Regrip R1).

    • Compare neighboring phases (for example, Over Bending and Retract) and check axis values. A large jump or sign change in joint axes is a common indicator of an unreachable posture.

    • In the Waypoints panel, adjust the Retract waypoint axes until the robot icon changes from infeasible to feasible.

    • Use the Simulate slider to verify that motion between phases is continuous and that no new collisions are introduced.

    • Recheck adjacent steps using Prev/Next before finalizing the fix.

  11. Collision error: A collision error indicates that the robot or gripper path interferes with tooling (for example, the die) at the selected step.

    • In the bend navigator tab, select the highlighted step and read the warning details (for example, Robot collides with die or Gripper collides with die).

    • click on the gripper, adjust Gripper Position and Angle.

    • Apply small incremental changes and check the robot pose after each update. Large edits can remove one collision but create another.
